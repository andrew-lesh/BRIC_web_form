Send your name to the ISS!  

Fill out this form by Monday, August 23rd, 2021 and your name and message will be included on the BRIC payload!



On December 1, 2021, the Stanford Student Space Initiative's Biopolymer Research for In-situ Capabilities (BRIC) payload will fly to the International Space Station, where it will test the formation of biopolymer-bound soil composite, a sustainable concrete-like building material, in microgravity. This carbon-neutral material, made from soil and a small amount of protein binder and water, may one day be used to make landing pads, habitats, and cities on Earth, the Moon, Mars, and other planets.

Want to know more? Check out our articles on the Stanford Daily and NASA's website!

Parent/guardian signatures are solely collected to confirm parent/guardian approval for submission of full names of persons under thirteen years of age and will not be stored aboard the payload. Interested parents/guardians are encouraged to submit their own names separately. No publication or sale of the collected data will be made. The BRIC experiment team members reserve the right to exclude any submitted information that is found to be harrassing or inappropriate.
